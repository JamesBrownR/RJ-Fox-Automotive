How a CVT Is Different from a Conventional Automatic

A conventional automatic transmission shifts through a fixed set of gears using clutch packs and bands. A CVT has no fixed gears at all. Instead it uses a pair of variable-diameter pulleys connected by a steel belt or chain. By continuously adjusting the pulley diameters, the CVT can provide any ratio within its range rather than stepping between set gear ratios.

The result is a transmission that keeps the engine in its most efficient RPM range at all times, which is good for fuel economy and smooth acceleration. The tradeoff is that the internal components operate under different stresses than a conventional automatic, and the fluid requirements are significantly more specific.

CVT fluid is formulated to lubricate the steel belt or chain and the pulley surfaces, maintain the correct friction characteristics for belt grip, and protect the variator components under the loads they experience. Conventional automatic transmission fluid does not do these things properly. Using the wrong fluid in a CVT can cause belt slipping, shuddering, premature wear, and in severe cases, catastrophic failure.

What CVT Service Includes

CVT service is not identical across all makes and models because manufacturers use different fluid specifications and have different service procedures. Here is what a CVT fluid service at RJ Fox Automotive covers.

  • Identification of the correct fluid specification for your specific year, make, and model
  • Drain and inspection of the old fluid for color, smell, and contamination
  • Pan removal and inspection where the design allows for it
  • Filter service where applicable
  • Refill with the manufacturer-specified CVT fluid at the correct quantity
  • External leak inspection at the pan, cooler lines, and seals
  • Road test to verify smooth operation and check for shudder, hesitation, or unusual behavior

Some CVTs do not have a serviceable filter or a traditional pan. In those cases the service focuses on a complete fluid exchange. We will explain what the service looks like for your specific vehicle before we start.

How Often Does a CVT Need Service?

CVT service intervals vary by manufacturer, and some automakers have published intervals that we think are longer than they should be based on what we see in the shop. Here is a practical guide.

  • Nissan CVT: Nissan's published interval has been as long as 60,000 miles for normal conditions, but Nissan CVTs have a well-documented history of problems related to fluid degradation. We recommend every 30,000 to 40,000 miles, and sooner if you do any towing or notice any shuddering

Warning Signs Your CVT Needs Attention

CVTs can fail suddenly, but they usually provide warning signs first. Pay attention to any of the following.

  • Shuddering or vibration during acceleration, especially at low speeds or when the transmission is cold; this is one of the most common early symptoms of CVT fluid degradation
  • Hesitation or surging when you press the accelerator, as if the transmission is hunting for the right ratio
  • Whining or buzzing noise from the transmission area, particularly under load
  • Overheating indicated by a warning light or the transmission going into a reduced power or limp mode
  • Jerking or lurching at low speeds that feels out of character for a CVT, which should be extremely smooth
  • Check engine or transmission warning lights with codes related to the CVT or ratio control

CVT shudder in particular is worth taking seriously early. It often responds well to a fluid exchange when caught at the first signs. Left alone, it typically progresses to the point where a fluid change alone will not resolve it.

The Risk of Using the Wrong Fluid

This is worth repeating because we see the consequences of it. Some quick lube shops and less experienced shops use a universal ATF or a generic CVT fluid in vehicles that require a specific OEM-spec fluid. This is a real problem for certain makes, particularly Nissan and Subaru, where the manufacturer's fluid formulation is significantly different from any generic substitute.

If your CVT has been serviced somewhere else and you are not sure what fluid was used, bring it in. We can check the fluid condition and give you an honest read on where things stand.
